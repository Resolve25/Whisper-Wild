Context

Why grooming can trigger meltdowns

Many children with autism and sensory processing differences experience nail care as a high-intensity sensory event: sound, vibration, pressure, and unpredictability all stack together. Our engagement-first design gives children predictability and choice—reducing fear responses and increasing cooperation.

Current stats

What we’re designing for

  • Autism prevalence: ~1 in 31 U.S. children, with ~85% experiencing sensory processing disorders (SPD)
  • Sensory processing challenges outside Autism: estimated at ~5–16% of all children
  • Households impacted: millions of families navigating daily care routines with added sensory load

Estimates vary by year and methodology; figures shown reflect commonly cited ranges.

What kids report feeling

Common triggers during nail trims

  • Sound: clipping or buzzing perceived as sharp or threatening
  • Vibration & pressure: unexpected micro-movements amplify discomfort
  • Smell & touch: clinical cues raise anxiety before the trim starts
  • Loss of control: “done to me” vs. “I choose” increases stress

Whisper Wild’s approach: predictable cues, playful framing, safe rounded blades, and an opt-in flow to reduce perceived threat.

Design principles

Engagement before action

  • Warm, animal-themed companion replaces clinical tools
  • Predictable, low-intensity lights & sounds (with mute)
  • Short, child-led steps: look → touch → practice → trim
  • Clean finish: integrated nail catcher reduces lingering mess
